Highlights


First of all, look at the design of Nokia 6.1 Plus in the above photo. It looks very premium with the smaller notch on top, 93% glass surface area and slim build. The frames are made of aluminum metal and the rear side has a glass. A 3rd generation gorilla glass protects the front glass. So, it is quite impressive in the design fact. But an aluminum back would have been more premium than a glass back and the glass will catch some finger spots that you need to clean now and then. 5.8″ display is pretty big, but with this kind of edge-to-edge design, a 6-inch device will easily fit in the pocket as well. So, It does have a few minor cons regarding design.

Now, here comes the big deal. Performance is the most attractive part of Nokia 6.1 Plus. A 4 GB RAM, highly optimized Android One (Oreo), Snapdragon 636 chipset, 1.8 GHz CPU and Andreo 509 GPU should make your phone smoother than you may expect from a typical mid-range phone. When the name is “Nokia”, you are extra sure about the hardware and software quality. Both 16 MP front and 16+5 MP dual back cameras are better than expected. Again, because the name is Nokia, you will see that extra fine details, smoothness, balanced brightness and sharpness in your photos which is often lower in many competitive Chinese brand phones even with higher megapixels. But of course, you can’t expect the flagship Samsung Galaxy, iPhone or Google Pixel series photo quality in Nokia 6.1 Plus. You can record 4K videos and EIS is available to Full HD video recording which stabilized the video. Overall, great design, performance and camera for the price.

Then comes the Ozo audio with three mics for clear audio on both sides of the call. 4GB RAM, 64GB ROM, Android Oreo, Adreno 512 GPU, octa-core CPU, UHD video, OTG, fingerprint and a powerful 3800 mAh battery with up to two days of backup for frequent users. You can confidently call it a masterpiece. No, we didn’t find any disadvantages. Although, some might prefer this lovely piece to be waterproof.
