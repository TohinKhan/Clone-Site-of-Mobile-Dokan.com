Highlights


Nokia worked hard on Nokia 1 to give users a smart, slightly individual and compact android experience. If you have some ideas about low priced smartphones, you will see the small and precise details Nokia is offering in Nokia 1.

First of all, Nokia 1 comes with 4G support. It is essential as now more and more calls are being made over the internet. Facebook, YouTube, WhatsApp, Snapchat, etc. need a fast internet connection, and they are the heart of a smartphone. Also, browsers will respond much faster with a 4G connection while browsing on the net. So, 4G is crucial for a modern phone. Then, there is YouTube Go. It will run much faster and use less data as you watch videos or stream your own. You can reduce playback quality to speed up the loading time and save data. Even downloading for the offline watch is also quite fast on YouTube Go. Nokia 1 also comes with the Android 8.0 Oreo Go edition. It will reduce lag while using apps. Built-in apps are already optimized to take less internal space and data. The chrome data saver will help to save up to 60% data. But so far, we have been talking about the software part. Now comes the hardware and you will see some downsides here.

5 MP camera with flash only (no autofocus), 2 MP front camera with no flash, 4.5-inch low-quality FWVGA display, and 480p video quality are weak specs. The body is colorful with the exchangeable Xpress-on covers, but it is made of cheap plastic. Although, the RAM, ROM, GPU, and processor will ensure fine performance. Together with Android Oreo Go and 4G connection, you should have overall fast experience. Regular gaming and multitasking should be no issue. 8 GB ROM will provide enough space for installing all your important apps and a decent amount of multimedia files without needing an extra MicroSD card. So, it is indeed a convenient phone concerning software and offers stable performance but has significant lacking in the richness of features.
