Highlights


First of all, Nokia 3.1 is an extraordinary name in the history of smartphones. “.” or point is usually used for software version in the tech industry. It is the first time a mobile phone itself is using “.” in its model name as far as our knowledge goes. However, it can be confusing for many and they may think that Nokia 3.1 is not a new phone but the old Nokia 3 with a software update. We were among them who were puzzled. So, our suggestion to Nokia would be not to name their models like this. But let us get into its features now.

Nokia 3.1 comes with a stylish design. The body is made of plastic, but the frames are made of aluminum. The display is a 5.2” trendy full-view display. But you may notice in the photo that there is way too much unnecessary space on the bottom which makes the design a little less attractive as far as minimal modern design is concerned. When it comes to performance, you can’t complain that much. 3 GB RAM, MediaTek MT6750 octa-core CPU, Mali-T860 MP2 GPU, 4G network support, 2990 mAh battery with up to 18 hours 3G talk-time and Android Oreo (Android One based) may ensure smooth and satisfactory performance in all aspects of your phone. Here to mention that Android One will ensure better performance and less power consumption by optimizing apps in the background, frequently scan your installed apps to provide extra security and so on which are some additional advantages in this device. The camera is ok, but it’s more on the downside.


A bigger display, slightly better camera, a bigger battery, Full HD video recording etc. could make this gadget a little more attractive to Nokia fans.
