Highlights

Nokia 3.2 is a stylish smartphone in the low-mid range scene. The design stands out quite well in the crowd. The display is big, rounded shape, logo placement etc. gives a very individualistic and elegant first impression. It comes with all the necessary features you may expect from a low-mid range device. One of the most significant advantages of Nokia 3.2 is its battery. The gadget comes with a 4000 mAh battery with 10W fast charging facility. Additionally, the phone is powered by Qualcomm’s Snapdragon 429 chipset, which is quite power efficient. On top of that, the device runs on Android One out of the box. It makes the phone even more power efficient. One can easily go for two days with one single charge. Android 9 Pie based on Android One is one of the most attractive features of Nokia 3.2. Usually, you won’t get Android One phones around this price range. For those who don’t know, Android One makes gives a very simplistic, fast and clean user interface. It also provides much faster Android updates. There is 3 GB RAM but the device runs on a Quad-core processor. So, you won’t get top-notch performance. It is still pretty neat and smooth for everyday usage. It is surely not the best gaming phone in the market, considering the price. But it certainly beats many of its competitors. Also, if longevity is important to you when it comes to performance, this is one of the better choices. There is a single 13 MP back camera and 5 MP front camera which may sound disappointing to many. Especially because most phones around the cost are coming with dual or even triple rear camera setup. 5 MP front camera also seems pretty inferior. But one shouldn’t forget that the name is still Nokia. So, it can’t be a total disappointment. Nokia’s software optimization speaks for itself. Even with lower megapixels and simple setup, it should deliver impressive shots. Fingerprint sensor, FM radio, biometric face unlock, 32 GB internal storage are some of its other essential features. Overall, Nokia 3.2 is a recommended phone from us. But you should always check out the phone by yourself and compare with other devices before finalizing your decision.
